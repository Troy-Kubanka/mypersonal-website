Software Background

In my journey, my exposure to software development and computer science began in college, as I hadn't encountered them before. Despite this, my early love for mathematics, which fostered critical thinking and a sense of reward, paved the way. High school then introduced me to physics, blending math with real-world applications, designs, and calculations. I enjoyed combining mathematical thinking with practical logic, so I enrolled in college as a declared physics major.

My first semester had courses in computer science, math, and physics. I immediately went from zero programming exposure to working with Python, C, and C++. Other upper-level students already had software development exposure from school, work, or family. I felt out of place, but in hindsight, this was a good thing because it challenged me to work harder. I spent a lot of extra time on my coursework, and learning computer science went from my most difficult subject to my favorite subject. The potential fell endless. After my first semester, I committed to majoring in computer science, and strive to be the best in my class.

Today I am eager to continue my interests and use my skills in the tech field. I enjoy understanding the computer science fundamentals while also learning up-to-date technologies. It is rewarding, exciting, and always evolving. My only regret is that I did not start sooner.
